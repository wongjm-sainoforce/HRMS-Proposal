01 · The Opportunity

Three moves to make our HR AI smarter — and safer

Our HRMS has started using AI. These three initiatives let us do more with it while keeping cost and quality firmly in our control. Each reuses systems we already run and is delivered in phases — so value lands early and risk stays low.

02 · Plan 1 — AI Usage & Cost Dashboard

See — and control — every ringgit and token

!The problem today

  • Spend is split across providers.To see costs we log into each provider's site separately — no single view.
  • One shared key for everything.We can't tell which feature — resume parsing, comparison, chatbot — spends what.
  • No per-person view.We can't allocate cost fairly or coach heavy users.
  • No early warning.A runaway feature could quietly run up a large bill before anyone notices.

✓What it is

  • One dashboard inside HRMSStyled like the familiar OpenAI usage page — no new tool to learn.
  • Spend, tokens & requestsBroken down by user, by module, and by API key.
  • Alerts & soft capsNotify at 80% of budget; optionally block at 100% until reviewed.
  • Admin-only (HR / HOD)Uses the access rules HRMS already has.
💡 Good news: the usage data we need is already produced by every AI call today — we simply aren't recording it yet. Capturing it is straightforward and reuses the systems we already have.

03 · Plan 2 — Agentic AI Assistant (LEXA)

From a policy bot to a multi-skill HR assistant

The opportunity

Today LEXA answers only company policy & Malaysian law. But most real HR questions cross boundaries:

"Can we rescind this candidate's offer — and what does the law say?"

That touches recruitment and the law. Right now a person has to stitch these together by hand.

One shared memory is the key advantage — skills pass context to each other and remember the conversation.

04 · Plan 3 — Self-Service Knowledge Ops

Safe, self-service updates to what the chatbot knows

!The problem today

  • Manual, developer-only chore.HR waits in a queue; simple policy updates take longer than they should.
  • No versioning.No clean record of what changed when, and no easy rollback.
  • Stale-content risk.Leftover fragments of an old version can stay live and be quoted by the bot.
  • No audit trail.Hard to show, for compliance, who approved what going live.

✓A controlled assembly line, with inspection points

A document enters; the system cleans, structures and prepares it; a person approves each stage; only approved content goes live.
